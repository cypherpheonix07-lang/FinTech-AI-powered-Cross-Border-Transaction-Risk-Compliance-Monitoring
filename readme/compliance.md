# 🛡️ PathGuard: Compliance & Regulatory Framework
## *Building Trust through Cryptographic Assurance*

PathGuard is designed not just to move money, but to move **responsibility and trust**. Our platform is architected to exceed global regulatory requirements by automating compliance at every hop.

---

### ⚖️ 1. Global Standards Alignment
PathGuard maps its core features directly to the most stringent international financial guidelines.

- **FATF Travel Rule**: Automatically shares and verifies originator and beneficiary information across border hops without stalling transactions.
- **GDPR / CCPA**: Privacy-by-design ensures that sensitive personal data stays local or is securely hashed, while the audit ledger remains cryptographically verifiable.
- **AML/KYC Enforcement**: Real-time GNN (Graph Neural Network) analysis identifies money laundering patterns (smurfing, layering) as they happen.

---

### 📜 2. The Verification "Passport"
Every transaction processed by PathGuard receives a digital "passport" that includes:
1. **Source Verification**: Confirmation of sender's KYC status.
2. **Hop Integrity**: Cryptographic proof that each bank/gateway has signed the transaction bundle.
3. **Risk Score**: A 0-100 score generated by the local PathGuard Sentinel.
4. **Audit Hash**: A unique Merkle proof linked to the immutable ledger.

---

### 🔍 3. Institutional Reporting
PathGuard automates the most time-consuming aspects of compliance.

- **Auto-SAR (Suspicious Activity Reports)**: Any transaction exceeding high-risk thresholds automatically triggers a standardized report draft with full forensic evidence.
- **Regulatory Dashboards**: Secure portals where authorized regulators can verify the health of the entire payment ecosystem in real-time.
- **Chain of Custody**: The immutable nature of the Merkle chained ledger ensures that evidence for investigations is tamper-proof.

---

### 🛡️ 4. Security Philosophy: No Opaque Hops
Traditional finance relies on "Blind Trust" (trusting the next bank will behave). PathGuard replaces this with **"Verifiable Trust"**:
> "If you cannot prove the hop is safe, PathGuard treats it as a roadblock."
